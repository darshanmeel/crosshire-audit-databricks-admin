# Jobs & Pipelines (Lakeflow)

The reliability, ownership, timeout hygiene and cost of Jobs and Lakeflow pipelines. Failures and wasted DBUs, all-purpose placement, missing timeouts, queueing, cold starts, retries and orphans.

📖 **Full interactive docs → [every query, explained](https://learn.crosshire.ch/learn/tech/databricks/audit#d-jobs)** — why it matters, what it does, how to read every output column, a sample of the result, and the caveats.

| # | Query | What it does |
|--:|---|---|
| 01 | [`lakeflow_failed_jobs_wasted_dbus`](https://learn.crosshire.ch/learn/tech/databricks/audit#q-lakeflow_failed_jobs_wasted_dbus) ★ | One row per job that had at least one failed run in the trailing window, with its distinct/failed run counts, the latest failure's termination code, total job DBUs, and a wasted-DBU proxy (job DBUs scaled by failed-run share). |
| 02 | [`lakeflow_jobs_on_all_purpose`](https://learn.crosshire.ch/learn/tech/databricks/audit#q-lakeflow_jobs_on_all_purpose) ★ | Per-job, per-cluster task-run counts over the trailing 30 days, tagged with the cluster's source so you can spot job tasks running on all-purpose (UI/API) compute instead of cheaper job clusters. |
| 03 | [`lakeflow_failed_runs`](https://learn.crosshire.ch/learn/tech/databricks/audit#q-lakeflow_failed_runs) | A 30-day census of completed job runs bucketed by workspace, launch type, trigger, final outcome, and root-cause code, with raw-row, distinct-run, and failed-row counts for each bucket. |
| 04 | [`lakeflow_health_rule_coverage`](https://learn.crosshire.ch/learn/tech/databricks/audit#q-lakeflow_health_rule_coverage) | Per-workspace counts of active jobs that have a configured health rule versus none, with a separate bucket for jobs where the health_rules column has not been populated yet. |
| 05 | [`lakeflow_job_ownership_orphans`](https://learn.crosshire.ch/learn/tech/databricks/audit#q-lakeflow_job_ownership_orphans) | Per-workspace counts of active jobs whose creator or run-as identity is missing, whose creator and run-as differ (a handoff or service-principal pattern), or that look orphaned because no known identity runs them. |
| 06 | [`lakeflow_job_queue_time`](https://learn.crosshire.ch/learn/tech/databricks/audit#q-lakeflow_job_queue_time) | Per-job queue-wait time (total seconds and approximate p95 per run) over the trailing window, with a separate counter of runs whose queue time is unknown, so admins can find jobs that sit waiting on capacity before they start. |
| 07 | [`lakeflow_job_tasks_no_timeout`](https://learn.crosshire.ch/learn/tech/databricks/audit#q-lakeflow_job_tasks_no_timeout) | Per-workspace counts of active job tasks and how many of them have no (or zero) task-level timeout configured, with a separate bucket for the "not yet populated" NULL case. |
| 08 | [`lakeflow_jobs_no_timeout`](https://learn.crosshire.ch/learn/tech/databricks/audit#q-lakeflow_jobs_no_timeout) | Per-workspace counts of active jobs that have no (or zero) job-level timeout configured, alongside a separate "not yet populated" NULL bucket so the result never over-claims. |
| 09 | [`lakeflow_never_started_runs`](https://learn.crosshire.ch/learn/tech/databricks/audit#q-lakeflow_never_started_runs) | A per-job count of Databricks job runs that were terminated before they ever began executing, broken out by the termination reason, over the trailing 30 days. |
| 10 | [`lakeflow_phase_cold_start`](https://learn.crosshire.ch/learn/tech/databricks/audit#q-lakeflow_phase_cold_start) | Per-job phase totals (setup, queue, execution, cleanup, and overall run) plus 95th-percentile setup and queue times over the trailing 30 days, so you can see how much wall-clock time is startup and wait overhead versus real work. |
| 11 | [`lakeflow_pipeline_cost`](https://learn.crosshire.ch/learn/tech/databricks/audit#q-lakeflow_pipeline_cost) | One row per Lakeflow (DLT) pipeline over the trailing window, showing its net DBUs billed, its maintenance DBUs broken out separately, a list-price dollar estimate, and how many times it refreshed and for how long. |
| 12 | [`lakeflow_pipeline_idle_tail_duration`](https://learn.crosshire.ch/learn/tech/databricks/audit#q-lakeflow_pipeline_idle_tail_duration) | Per-Lakeflow-pipeline count of updates and total active run seconds over the last 30 days, tagged with each pipeline's type and its continuous/development settings so you can spot pipelines whose clusters likely linger idle between refreshes. |
| 13 | [`lakeflow_pipeline_update_failures_retries`](https://learn.crosshire.ch/learn/tech/databricks/audit#q-lakeflow_pipeline_update_failures_retries) | A 30-day breakdown of Lakeflow/DLT pipeline update outcomes, counting how many refreshes ran, how many failed, and how many were fired by retry-on-failure, sliced per workspace, pipeline, update type, trigger, and result state. |
| 14 | [`lakeflow_pipelines_inventory_tier`](https://learn.crosshire.ch/learn/tech/databricks/audit#q-lakeflow_pipelines_inventory_tier) | A per-workspace census of your live Lakeflow Declarative (DLT) pipelines, counting how many run in each combination of pipeline type, serverless, development-mode, continuous, and product edition. |
| 15 | [`lakeflow_retries_repairs`](https://learn.crosshire.ch/learn/tech/databricks/audit#q-lakeflow_retries_repairs) | Per-job retry tallies over the trailing 30 days — how many runs each job had, how many extra attempts those runs racked up, and how many runs needed at least one retry. |
| 16 | [`lakeflow_stale_zombie_jobs`](https://learn.crosshire.ch/learn/tech/databricks/audit#q-lakeflow_stale_zombie_jobs) | Every active (non-deleted) job definition with the timestamp its last run was last seen running and a flag marking those that haven't run in over 30 days (or ever). |
| 17 | [`lakeflow_succeeded_with_failed_tasks`](https://learn.crosshire.ch/learn/tech/databricks/audit#q-lakeflow_succeeded_with_failed_tasks) | Per job, how many runs reported overall SUCCEEDED yet actually contained at least one task that FAILED, ERROR'd, or TIMED_OUT over the trailing 30 days. |
| 18 | [`lakeflow_tasks_near_timeout`](https://learn.crosshire.ch/learn/tech/databricks/audit#q-lakeflow_tasks_near_timeout) | Per-task run statistics over the lookback window flagging task runs that reached at least 80% of (or exceeded) their configured timeout, so you can tune the bound or fix the slowdown before it trips. |
| 19 | [`lakeflow_termination_taxonomy`](https://learn.crosshire.ch/learn/tech/databricks/audit#q-lakeflow_termination_taxonomy) | A 30-day tally, per workspace, of how many job runs ended with each termination code — the root-cause taxonomy of why runs stopped. |
| 20 | [`lakeflow_termination_type_probe`](https://learn.crosshire.ch/learn/tech/databricks/audit#q-lakeflow_termination_type_probe) | A runtime discovery probe that lists the distinct raw `termination_type` values appearing on finished job runs per workspace over the last 30 days, with a count of how many end-of-run rows carry each value. |
| 21 | [`lakeflow_workload_mix_hours`](https://learn.crosshire.ch/learn/tech/databricks/audit#q-lakeflow_workload_mix_hours) | A daily breakdown, per workspace, of how many distinct job runs were active and how many execution-seconds they consumed, sliced by run type and trigger type over the trailing 30 days. |

<sub>★ = first-audit pick. This is a one-line index — the full write-up (output columns, sample rows, caveats) lives in the [interactive docs](https://learn.crosshire.ch/learn/tech/databricks/audit). The `.sql` files in this folder are the source of truth.</sub>
